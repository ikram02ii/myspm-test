conceptTitle: Ion present in aqueous copper(II) chloride solution
content:
In aqueous copper(II) chloride (CuClΓéé), the compound dissociates completely into its constituent ions: Cu┬▓Γü║(aq) and ClΓü╗(aq). ### chunk_index=30 chunk_id=chunk-31
conceptTitle: Half-equation at the anode for copper(II) chloride electrolysis (Set I & III)
content:
For Set I (inert electrodes, e.g., carbon), the anode half-equation is: 2ClΓü╗(aq) ΓåÆ ClΓéé(g) + 2eΓü╗. For Set III (copper electrodes), oxidation of the copper anode occurs: Cu(s) ΓåÆ Cu┬▓Γü║(aq) + 2eΓü╗. This distinction hinges on electrode reactivity: inert anodes allow anion oxidation (ClΓü╗), while reactive metal anodes dissolve preferentially. Candidates must correctly assign equations based on electrode material ΓÇö a common error is applying the same equation across all sets without considering electrode composition.

### chunk_index=31 chunk_id=chunk-32
conceptTitle: Observation of solution colour change during electrolysis of CuClΓéé (Set I vs Set III)
content:
In Set I (inert electrodes), the blue colour of Cu┬▓Γü║(aq) fades gradually as Cu┬▓Γü║ is reduced at the cathode and ClΓü╗ oxidised at the anode ΓÇö no new Cu┬▓Γü║ is generated, so [Cu┬▓Γü║] decreases. In Set III (copper electrodes), the blue colour remains unchanged because Cu┬▓Γü║ lost at the cathode is replenished by dissolution of the copper anode (Cu ΓåÆ Cu┬▓Γü║ + 2eΓü╗), maintaining constant ion concentration. Accurate observation requires linking electrode behaviour to ion balance and visual cues (intensity/fading of blue hue).

conceptTitle: Products formed at the anode in different electrolytic setups (Set I, II, III) and effect of concentration change
content:
At the anode: Set I (inert, 0.001 mol dmΓü╗┬│ CuClΓéé) yields ClΓéé(g); Set II (inert, dilute NaΓééSOΓéä) yields OΓéé(g) from OHΓü╗ oxidation; Set III (copper electrodes) yields Cu┬▓Γü║(aq) via anode dissolution.
